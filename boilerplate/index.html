Wisconsin Cows

Bryce Linden

The Problem

America's dairy industry is struggling with Wisconsin farmers especially feeling the heat. In 2018, over 2700 dairy farms closed in the US with over a third in Wisconsin. As more young people move away from the farming livestyle, the future of dairy in Wisconsin is threatened.

The History

Wisconsin's love affair with dairy began at the end of the 19th century when soil exhaustion from wheat farming pushed farmers to try dairy. By the turn of the century, nearly 90% of farms had switched to dairy.

The Data

All data in this map comes from the US Department of Agriculture survey for Wisconsin. The data covers 30 years of Wisconsin's dairy history with data collected in 5 year intervals. The 15 counties selected represent the counties where most dairy cows can be found. Currently, Clark county in the northwest leads the way with over 66,000 head, outnumbering people over 2 to 1.
